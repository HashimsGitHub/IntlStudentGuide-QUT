International Student Journey at QUT

A comprehensive guide for international students arriving at Brisbane International Airport, settling in Brisbane, and succeeding at Queensland University of Technology.

Arrival at Brisbane International Airport

Please Read Carefully the Incoming Passenger Card . This is a Government issued document (the same folks who issued you your Student Visa), so the information provided in IPC gets stored in your visa file. While the Torres Strait Islander people first settled in their islands around 4,000 years ago, your Incoming Passenger Card travel history is also retained upto 4,000 years too. You will need to provide a Contact Person's Name, their Phone or Email and your place of stay in Australia i.e. your address. This will help authorities and police contact that Person in case of your Emergency. For example, if something unfortunate happens to you such as a car accident or a medical emergency, the authorities will need to contact that Person which could be your relative living in Australia, a close family friend in the city, your accommodation provider's contact, or lastly a University contact person (if you really don't know anyone here).

Warning: People have had their visas refused and deported when they are caught lying on their IPC. Every time you travel internationally, you must submit an IPC. The IPC records will be pulled up at the time of your visa status change (PR, Citizenship, etc.).

Biosecurity Critical

Please take very seriously the pre-arrival Biosecurity video shown in aircraft before landing, and do not bring any food, seeds, plants, herbs, soil or any such item that might harm the environment.

Seriously: Your foodstuff can cause the continent to go extinct. So, your Grandmother's recipe or spices - kindly leave them back in your home country. Otherwise, the Airport Biosecurity officers/customs will impose hefty fines and also destroy all your foodstuff.

Food items, herbs, seeds, foreign soil may kill wildlife. But if the same wildlife comes in front of your car on a highway, you will have no option but to run them over. And yes, grocery stores sell Kangaroo meat.

Communications

You are excited to land in Australia after a long trip and first thing you want to do is communicate back home to your family that you have landed safely.

Good thing is that Brisbane Airport has free Wi-Fi. Alternatively, if you are really travel smart then you must have purchased international roaming eSIM like AloSIM or Airalo.

There are some mobile SIM provider stalls at the airport, but it depends on the arrival time of your flight and whether they are open, or if it is a weekend. Opt for a pre-paid SIM if you are unsure which provider and plan is best for you. Your passport is connected to many systems so as soon as you give your passport details, the system will verify your visa status (don't try to use nicknames otherwise visa / VEVO check cannot be done by these systems).

Telstra, Optus, and Vodafone are reputed mobile providers in Australia, however, make sure your Phone is 4G and 5G supported, and not 3G as that will not work in here.

P.S. For those who like vanity mobile numbers ,Amaysim is mobile SIM provider that sells custom mobile numbers at reasonable price (it uses Optus Network).

Transport from Airport

Now that you have landed at the airport, freshened up, took some selfies, drank your very expensive airport cafe coffee or matcha, bought some really expensive water from the airport shop - you then realize now you need to go to the city.

For those who don't have any relatives (sometimes entire tribes) to come pick them up from the airport, who need to arrange transport on their own, there are a few options:

  • Maxi Taxi (13Cabs App): For those who have so much luggage that won't fit in an SUV. Maxi Taxi are customized Toyota Hiace vans modded to carry very large amounts of baggage.
  • Uber App: For loyal Uber customers.
  • Didi App: A cheaper alternative to Uber.
  • AirTrain: While the train is the same as Brisbane's Queensland Rail SEQ Network trains, the tracks going from airport to the city are platinum plated! The train from Brisbane Airport to the city is 45 times more expensive than the regular city ($22 for AirTrain and $0.50 for Normal Train One-Way). Please check AirTrain site or at the Ticket Counter of Train Station located at the Airport for fares to the City.
  • $1 Hack: Technically it is possible to reach the City or even QUT in $1, but you must have very limited luggage so it's easy for you to hop/change buses and trains to hack the system. Start your journey by taking the Airport Free Shuttle bus to DFO. From DFO there take the 590 Bus to Toombul ($0.50) . Make sure you get onboard from the correct bus stop which is the first one in the direction of travel of the bus , so basically the first stop of 590 and not the second one, otherwise you will be in for your first ride misadventure. Then from Toombul Interchange take the Bus to your Destination or cross the road to catch the Train. Total trip cost $1.00!

    • Remeber one day when you get old with grey hair and leaving Australia you will come here to buy branded items for your relatives back home from DFO (Direct Factory Outlet)

P.S. Always use Google Maps to plan your journey! Note that the extremely cheap method ($1 hack) is not feasible at very late hours or when some bus operators don't work. Also, don't expect Cinderella's Chariot waiting for you at the airport at midnight!

Public Transport in Brisbane a.k.a Translink

Some Queensland politician promised 50 cents fare to win the election, but he still lost it! However, Australians never break their promise- So yes, we have permanent 50-cent fare on all public transport (South East Queensland Network). So you can take bus, train and ferry and pay only 50 cents.

You can purchase the Translink card from the train station at the airport or from the Central Train Station in the Central Business District (CBD is also called the City). Most buses, train stations, ferries are now fitted with VISA/MasterCard enabled readers so even if you don't get a Translink card then use your bank cards (Credit or Debit card). Here is the Translink Map.

Don't be surprised to see a 10c charge on your credit card thinking you outsmarted the system and are paying only 10c as that is just pre-authorization. The fare is still 50 cents and you will get the deduction next business day.

Want to take the world's most unnecessary 50-cent day trip? Go from Coolangatta to Gympie! That's a real trip you can do for "fifty cents". That's over 250 km of uninterrupted scenery, podcasts, and awkward eye contact with strangers for 6+ hours.

Australian Address Essential

Everything in Australia is tied to your residential address, right from filling up the Incoming Passenger Card to buying a Mobile SIM at the Airport, opening a Bank Account or applying at any Government service office for a Driver's License or a Photo ID card. Address is mandatory so please provide your Australian address - it can be a temporary one and you can update it later.

I am sure you are not going to sleep on the streets and be homeless on Day-One. So, go ahead and use your Brisbane address, even if it's your Hotel, Airbnb, Student accommodation, Hostel, relative or a close friend.

Important: For Bank cards or any Government official correspondence letters and cards (Medicare, Photo ID etc.), don't give out Airbnb or hotel address if you will be checking-out earlier than the arrival of that item, otherwise you will get bankrupt very soon if someone else receives your ATM card in their post. Best is to give an address where you can gaurantee you will recieve it.

Yes, Government and Banks work on good old Postal system. People are planning for Mars but Australia uses Snail-Mail i.e. Australia Post because the government believes only humans can have real postal addresses and not AI bots.

P.S. There is an alternative approach to rent a Virtual Mailbox Address if you don't have a physical address with a lease contract in your name so you can get your mail delivered there.

Banking in Australia First Week

To open a bank account you need Australian mobile number, email, Australian address, visa grant, VEVO visa status check (sometimes needed) and then stand in long queues. Banks use BSB Number and Account Number (No SWIFT , IBAN etc) so ensure your overseas bank supports this for international wire transfers.

Banks issue a Digital debit card which you can immediately link and activate to your Apple or Google Wallet, but the physical card will be delivered via Australia Post which takes 1-2 weeks.

Activate your Digital Wallet and till you get your physical ATM card, tap your phone on all the POS (Point of Sale) terminals. Once you get your physical card, good news is that the Debit Card is VISA/MasterCard enabled so it will work everywhere (even for all online purchases).

Forex / Currency Exchange Rates: If you are getting very poor AUD exchange rate in your home country then just bring USD, EUR or GBP or any globally recognized currency. There is a foreign exchange broker at Brisbane Airport - International Terminal Prosegur Change plus there are many Travelex shops in the city. Furthermore, once you reach the city certain banks allow you to open USD account which you can later transfer to your personal AUD account via the Banks's App whenever the exchange rate is good.

Checking vs Savings at ATM - (more confusing than the movie Inception) ! Every bank by default will open a Checking Account and a Savings Account, and because its Australia so everything has to be upside-down. When you withdraw cash from your ATM you will need to select Savings Account and similarly when you Deposit again you need to select Savings Account, however, the transactions will happen with your Checking Account ! In Australia, it is common for ATMs and payment terminals to label your Checking (Transaction) account as "Savings" or "Cheque". This is largely a legacy of the Australian EFTPOS system rather than a reflection of your sanity .

Credit Card: FORGET IT! Australian banks do not issue credit cards until you have a JOB and submit 3-6 months of income statements & payslips. Only then you will be eligible for a Credit Card and will get a Credit Score.

P.S. There is a trick to open a bank account without even visiting the branch (branchless banking with Mobile Apps such as ANZ Plus, UBank, UpBank). However, you do need to upload from your mobile your Passport, Visa, Proof of Address and an Australian Mobile number. Otherwise good luck standing for 4 hours in CommBank queue, or getting an appointment at a bank (Westpac, HSBC, NAB etc) - yes banks have appointment system!

Caution: Some banks like Commonwealth Bank advertise that you can open a Bank Account overseas 14 days prior to your arrival. Whilst this is true but the fineprint is that they open a Frozen Account, so any money you deposit via wire transfer from overseas will bounce back ! You will need to physically show up to the CommBank, verify your identity using Passport, Visa Grant , Proof of Address etc. to unfreeze that account. Only advantage is that your ATM card will be ready.

Culture Shock in Australia Reality Check

Australia is not just geographically upside down - many things here will surprise you! Here are some culture shocks that international students commonly experience:

Electrical Power - 220-240V Only!

Australia uses 220-240V AC 50Hz electrical power with Type I plugs (three flat pins). If your electronics are designed for 110V (common in USA, Japan, etc.), you'll need a voltage converter, not just a plug adapter!

Australian Type I Power Socket (3-pin)

Warning: Plugging 110V devices directly into 240V outlets without a converter will fry your electronics instantly! Check your device specifications before plugging in.

Cigarettes & Tobacco - Prepare Your Wallet!

Australia has some of the highest tobacco prices in the world. A pack of 20 cigarettes costs around $40-$60 AUD (yes, you read that right!).

The government imposes heavy excise taxes to discourage smoking. If you're a smoker, this might be a good opportunity to quit! Otherwise, your smoking habit will cost you more than your rent.

The Barefoot Culture

Don't be shocked if you see Australians walking barefoot in shopping centres, supermarkets, or even on public transport! It's surprisingly common, especially in Queensland's warm climate.

While it might seem unusual to international students, many locals embrace this casual, laid-back lifestyle. You'll see people barefoot at:

  • Beach suburbs and coastal areas (very common)
  • Shopping centres and supermarkets
  • Parks and outdoor venues
  • Sometimes even in the CBD!

P.S. While it's legal and socially acceptable, most businesses reserve the right to refuse service to barefoot customers for safety reasons. But realistically, nobody really cares!

Sticker Shock - Expensive Prices

Australia has a high cost of living. Here are some prices that might shock you:

  • Coffee: $4.50-$7 for a regular cappuccino or latte
  • Dining Out: $20-$35 for a basic meal at a casual restaurant
  • Cinema Tickets: $15-$25 per person (more for IMAX or Gold Class)
  • Haircut: $30-$60 for men, $60-$150+ for women
  • Gym Membership: $15-$80 per week depending on facilities
  • Domestic Flights: $100-$300 for short routes (Brisbane to Sydney)
  • Halal Food: Slightly more expensive than regular restaurants

Budget Tip: Cook at home, use student discounts wherever possible, and take advantage of free activities like beaches, parks, and hiking trails. Brisbane has plenty of free entertainment! Shop at KMart and BigW for budget friendly Clothing, Makeup, Household Items, Toiletries, Kitchen Utensils, Novels, Office Stationery, Electronics. Use Everyday Rewards and Flybuys Apps to get discount points. If you are into American style big bulk buying, then become Costco member.

7-Eleven has the cheapest coffee in the city, they have precision engineering built-in to their Coffee machines and it fills your cup to the brim with zero spillage (talk about nanometer accuracy) !

McDonald's has the cheapest Ice Cream cone 50 cents! , Woolworths (a.k.a Wooleys) and Coles will not sell single ice cream cone except bulk, but for that you need to visit Woolworths Metro or Coles Local , EzyMart etc. There is a Gelato craze and you will see huge queues around Ice-Cream Parlors.

Other Cultural Surprises

  • Tipping is NOT expected: Service staff earn proper wages, so tipping is optional and uncommon (unlike USA)
  • Everything closes early: Most shops close by 5-6 PM on weekdays (9 PM on Thursdays), except Thursday "late night shopping"
  • No one jaywalks: Australians actually wait for the green man at pedestrian crossings!
  • Wildlife encounters: Yes, you might see possums, wallabies, or even snakes. Stay calm and keep your distance
  • Casual dress code: Australians dress very casually. Thongs and shorts are acceptable almost everywhere
  • No bargaining: Prices are fixed. Don't try to negotiate except at markets or car yards
  • No Bidets: Most toilets in Australia do not have bidets. You'll need to use toilet paper for cleaning or BYOB , no pun intended.
  • Natural Habitat Preservation: Whilst there is great diversity and a multi-cultural society, locals culture is much preserved.
  • Woolworths and Coles: There are only two major supermarket chains in Australia.

QUT Student Success Guide Must Read

Student vs "Student"

While it is legally allowed to work part-time (as per conditions stated in your student visa), some students are really not here for studying. They dream of becoming billionaires so they think going to Uni is a waste of their precious time. They are chasing American dream but in the wrong continent!

While there is nothing wrong with side-hustle doing Uber Eats, making sandwiches in Subway or washing dishes at a restaurant, some students make their part-time work more important than their studies. They are the ones who get poor grades and sometimes get caught at the airport due to abuse of student visa.

Good students make full use of all the facilities provided by University. They use library which is open 24x7; they work as volunteers for HiQ, they are active in University student clubs, use spare time to learn extra skills and go the extra mile in learning.

Social Media Influencer vs Professional Networking

Yes, admit it; your Instagram will be filled with photos of places all over Australia! Some think Student Visa granted is a Tourist Visa grant, oblivious to the fact that you need to be eligible for TR (Temporary Residence) afterwards and PR (Permanent Residence) after that.

No one is interested in photos of your Matcha drinks or your visit to the Beach. But if you really want to be job-ready then participate in business events (seminars, tech talks, symposium, expos), build your LinkedIn networks and not Instagram followers.

Academic Success Tips at QUT

  • While lectures are recorded and available for viewing anytime on Canvas in Echo360, attend lectures in person. Your parents paid for you to study in Uni, so better attend face-to-face.
  • Must attend all tutorials in person - the attendance in tutorials is recorded and submitted to government.
  • Try to spend as much time as possible in the Uni during semester. Make notes for each class particularly paying interest to the topic or domain area mentioned by Professors.
  • There is no harm in part-time work but legally that's 4 hours per day. You still have the whole day so attend all classes and tutorials in-person.
  • Use the University account (email ID/Windows credentials) to install Office and OneDrive on your personal laptop. Keep all course materials and study work on Uni's OneDrive account so your dont lose it when you spill coffee (or Mactha these days) on your laptop. Plus you can access that OneDrive from any PC in the campus.
  • Install QUT, Canvas and Rubric App on your mobile. Having Canvas on mobile helps you keep an eye of submission deadlines and brush up on the course notes.
  • Never procrastinate or leave assignments, projects etc. till the last minute but rather try to do it as soon as it is released. Take hints for the Tutorial solutions till last week before deadline , as some content may make up part of your assignment.

Academic Integrity Warning: Never use AI or outsource your assignments. The University uses Turnitin with AI detection tools! Cheaters are reported to the Academic Integrity Council. You cannot outsmart your Professors—they too use AI to check your assignments for AI generated or plagiarised content.

Transport to GP Campus : QUT Gardens Point has its own Ferry terminal, so you can catch a Ferry to GP. There are two free City Loop buses, 40 (Clockwise route) and 50 (Anticlockwise route), both will drop you off to GP campus for $0.00 (Please dont tap On or Off in these Free Loop buses). The nearest Train Station to GP campus is South Bank, you just need to walk across the Goodwill bridge. For those coming from suburbs can get off at Central Station (if there is no South Bank stop) and walk across the Queen Street shopping district to GP campus

Transport to KG Campus : QUT Gardens Point has its own Bus terminal, however there is no ferry terminal or train station connecting to KG campus. The nearest train station would be Normanby from there you can do a long walk or take a bus to KG campus. Good thing is that the latest electric Metro Bus stops at KG bus terminal, among other buses. While there is no Free Loop bus for KG, but you can take QUT Shuttle bus between GP and KG campuses at zero cost.

Pro Tip: Use 'QUT' Wi-Fi when on Campus, plus if you connect to 'eduroam' then you can use your QUT Credentials to connect to Wi-Fi of any University throughout Australia wherever 'eduroam' Wi-Fi is available.

P.S.

There are EzyMart shops in GP and KG Campus that have machine dispensed coffee at really affordable prices as compared to any other coffee shop on campus, everything else there is at skyrocket prices though.

Both campuses have Food Courts , whilst GP is in the heart of the city (so you can go to several restaurants, cafes, malls, even Cinema) , KG has a small 'Village' that has many cafes, stores and restaurants.

Brisbane is home to many Weekend Flea Markets, you can get good bargain prices, GP students can go to Botanic Gardens market or Brisbane Square Library Markets and KG students can go to The Village.
